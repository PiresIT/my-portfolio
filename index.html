About Me

Background & Interests

I am a graduate researcher in International Public Policy at the University of Tsukuba, with a background spanning finance, audit, and data-driven policy analysis. My research focuses on the macroeconomic effects of foreign direct investment, with my thesis examining how industry-level FDI shapes economic growth across developing economies.

Prior to my graduate studies, I gained hands-on analytical experience as an Associate Auditor at EY (Big 4) in Tashkent, where I worked with financial data, internal controls, and evidence-based reporting. My undergraduate degree in Finance, Investment and Risk from Bangor University — completed with First Class Honours — grounded me in quantitative reasoning and financial analysis.

I am passionate about applying rigorous data science methods — including Python, R, Stata, SQL, and Tableau — to real-world policy questions. I hold certifications in Machine Learning, AI Ethics, and Cybersecurity, reflecting my commitment to bridging quantitative skills with policy relevance.
